What Is Letterpress Printing?

Letterpress is the oldest form of printing in the Western world — a 500-year-old technique that presses inked type or plates into soft paper, leaving a deep, tactile impression you can feel with your fingertips. In an age of flat, disposable printing, letterpress endures because it engages the sense of touch in a way nothing else can.

Why Cotton Paper Defines Fine Stationery

Crane Lettra is made from 100% cotton fiber — the same material used in currency paper. Unlike wood pulp, cotton won’t yellow or become brittle with age. It has a soft, weighty hand that feels substantial the moment you pick it up, and its fibers compress beautifully under the letterpress, holding that deep impression for decades.
