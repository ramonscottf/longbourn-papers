Hands-On Service

7 Ways to Serve

You don’t have to write a check to make a difference. Here are seven tangible ways you can directly support students and families across the district.

1

Teen Center Supplies

Over 1,300 DSD students experience homelessness each year. Teen Centers provide essential supplies—here’s what they need most.

Items Needed

  • Elementary boy socks
  • Elementary girl socks
  • Elementary boy underwear
  • Teen boy sweats
  • Teen boy underwear
  • Dish detergent
  • Laundry detergent
  • Quart size storage bags
  • Sandwich size storage bags
  • Snack size storage bags
  • Reusable grocery bags

Hygiene Items

  • Feminine hygiene liners
  • Feminine hygiene pads
  • Full size conditioner
  • Full size male deodorant
2

Pantry Packs

Pack shelf-stable meals for students to take home on weekends. 1 in 5 school-age children in Davis County is food insecure.

Each Pack Contains

  • 2 Macaroni and Cheese Packets
  • 2 Oatmeal Packets
  • Drink Packet
  • Fruit Snacks
  • Granola Bars
  • Crackers
  • Fruit Pouch
3

Meal & Grocery Gift Cards

Donate $15 gift cards to local grocery stores or restaurants. These go directly to students and families in crisis.

4

School Pantry Donations

Food pantries in our high schools help feed students and families in need. These are the items they need most:

  • Boxes of cereal (at least 10 oz. or more)
  • Shelf stable fruit (canned and individual packages)
  • Canned or pouched chicken
  • Canned and non-perishable food items
