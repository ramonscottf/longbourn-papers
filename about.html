Our Story

Born from a Love of Letters

Heirloom stationery crafted by hand, pressed with intention, and sent with heart.

The Beginning

A Single Impression Changed Everything

In the spring of 2019, Alexandra walked into a bookbinding class at a small arts center in Salt Lake City with no expectations. She left with an obsession.

It was the moment the press kissed the paper for the first time — that quiet, deliberate bite of metal into soft cotton — that something shifted. She ran her fingers across the impression and felt what no screen, no printer, no modern convenience could replicate: the physical weight of a word. The undeniable proof that someone had taken the time to say something that mattered.

Within weeks, Alexandra had cleared out the spare bedroom of her Farmington, Utah home and installed a tabletop press she found at an estate sale. The machine was heavy, temperamental, and magnificent. She named it Bingley — after the more agreeable of Jane Austen's gentlemen — and spent her evenings learning its rhythms, feeding it ink and cotton paper until her hands were stained and her heart was full.

The Obsession

From Spare Bedroom to Studio

What began as a quiet creative outlet quickly became an all-consuming pursuit of the perfect impression.

Alexandra spent months experimenting — mixing custom inks by hand, testing dozens of cotton papers from mills across America and Europe, adjusting pressure one fraction of a turn at a time. She was searching for something specific: that rare balance where the ink sits deep enough to feel beneath your thumb but light enough that every serif remains crisp. Where the paper yields just so, creating a relief that catches the light at certain angles, like a secret only the recipient would notice.

She began giving her cards to friends and family. A sympathy note for a neighbor. A birthday tag tied to a bottle of wine. A simple "thank you" pressed onto a cotton card no bigger than a playing card. People held onto them. They kept them on mantels, tucked them into journals, pinned them to kitchen boards. Nobody threw them away. That was when Alexandra knew she had found something worth building.

“In a world that moves at the speed of a notification, I wanted to make something that asked you to slow down — to feel the paper, to choose your words, to mean what you write.”

— Alexandra, Founder of Longbourn Papers
What We Believe

The Principles Behind Every Press

Intention

Every card we create exists to hold a moment of meaning. We design for the pause before the pen touches paper — that breath where you gather your thoughts and decide what truly needs to be said. Our stationery is not for dashing off quick notes. It is for the words that deserve to last.

Heritage

Letterpress printing is over five hundred years old. We honor that lineage by working with traditional presses, hand-mixed inks, and time-tested techniques passed down through generations of printers. Each impression connects the sender to a craft that predates the modern world — and reminds us that some things are better done slowly.

Quality

We use only premium 100% cotton paper, sourced from American mills that share our commitment to craft. Our inks are mixed by hand in small batches. Every print run is inspected piece by piece. We would rather make fewer pieces and make them right than ever compromise on what you hold in your hands.

Milestones

The Longbourn Journey

2019

The First Impression

Alexandra pulls her first letterpress print on a restored tabletop press in her Farmington, Utah home studio. Longbourn Papers is born — named for the Bennet family estate in Pride and Prejudice, a nod to the enduring power of the written word.

2020

First Collection Launches

In a year when the world slowed down, people rediscovered the art of staying connected through mail. Longbourn's debut collection of gift tags and petite cards sells out within weeks, fueled by word of mouth and a growing community of handwritten-note devotees.

2021

Wholesale Expansion

Independent boutiques and gift shops across the Mountain West begin carrying Longbourn Papers. The line expands to include sympathy cards, celebration stationery, and the signature cotton envelope sets that become the brand's hallmark.

2023

The Studio Move

Longbourn outgrows the spare bedroom and moves into a dedicated studio space — complete with a full-size Chandler & Price platen press, a proofing station, and room to dream bigger. The team grows to include two studio assistants who share Alexandra's reverence for the craft.

2025

National Recognition

Longbourn Papers is featured in national publications as a leader in the modern letterpress revival. The brand now ships to all fifty states and has become a trusted name among those who believe that how you say something matters just as much as what you say.
