Our Commitment

Why Early Literacy Matters

Reading is the single most important skill a child can develop. Research consistently shows that children who are read to at home develop stronger vocabularies, greater comprehension, and deeper curiosity about the world. But it goes even deeper than that—early literacy shapes confidence, self-expression, and a child’s belief in what’s possible.

Too many children arrive at kindergarten without ever owning a book of their own. That’s not a reflection of their families’ values—it’s an access problem. And it’s one the Davis Education Foundation is determined to help solve.

We believe that a child’s zip code, family income, or circumstances should never determine whether they grow up loving books. Every child in Davis County deserves the chance to hold a brand-new book in their hands, curl up with a parent or caregiver, and discover the magic of a story.

What This Program Does

Through our partnership with Dolly Parton’s Imagination Library, enrolled children receive a brand-new, age-appropriate book mailed directly to their home every single month—from birth until age five. That’s up to 60 books before a child ever walks into a classroom.

These aren’t just any books. Each title is carefully selected by a panel of early childhood literacy experts to spark imagination, develop language skills, and create meaningful moments between children and the adults who love them. The books arrive addressed to the child—because there is something powerful about a little one getting mail with their own name on it.

Official Local Partner

Dolly Parton’s Imagination Library

When Dolly Parton started the Imagination Library in 1995, she had one goal: make sure every child could have books in their home, regardless of their family’s income. What started in her hometown in Tennessee has grown into a worldwide movement that has gifted over 200 million books to children.

The Davis Education Foundation is proud to serve as a local champion for this program in Davis County. As the local affiliate partner, we fund and coordinate the program so families can participate at absolutely no cost.

The Process

How It Works

Children Are Enrolled

Families enroll their children through our launch partners, starting with Head Start. Enrollment is simple and free.

Books Arrive Monthly

A brand-new, age-appropriate book is mailed directly to the child’s home every month—addressed to them by name.

Home Libraries Grow

Over time, children build a personal library of up to 60 books—creating a powerful foundation for kindergarten readiness and a lifelong love of reading.

The Program Expands

As community support grows, we expand enrollment to reach more families across Davis County—because every child deserves this opportunity.

About the Imagination Library

Founded by Dolly Parton in 1995, the Imagination Library is dedicated to inspiring a love of reading by gifting books free of charge to children from birth to age five. The program now operates across the United States, Canada, UK, Australia, and Ireland.

Looking Ahead

Growing Beyond Head Start

Right now, our Imagination Library program serves children enrolled in Head Start. But our vision is much bigger. We want every child in Davis County to have access to this program—regardless of where they go to school or which programs their family participates in.

Phase 1: Head Start

We launched with Head Start families—children who benefit most from having books in the home. This is our foundation.

Phase 2: Expand Enrollment

As funding grows, we’ll open the program to additional families—connecting more children to their first home library.

Phase 3: Countywide Access

Our ultimate goal: every child in Davis County has the opportunity to receive free books at home from birth to age five.

Expanding this program takes community support. Monthly giving is the fastest, most responsible way to help us reach more families and put more books into more homes.
