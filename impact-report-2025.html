Foundation Highlights

A Year of Impact

From summer kickoffs to spring celebrations, every season brought moments that mattered.

Summer
9/11 Day of Service
Wards and stakes delivered months of gathered essentials, filling the warehouse for the year ahead. The Foundation earned five Best of State category awards and the Best of State Statue for Community Development.
Child Spree
Donors and volunteers paired with students for a back-to-school shopping experience. Over 500 students began the school year with clothing, shoes and confidence.
Discover Davis & Stuff the Bus
New teachers explored district resources, and Stuff the Bus rallied the community to raise at least $10,000 and fill a real school bus with supplies.
Fall
FORE Kids Golf Tournament
A sold-out tournament at Oakridge Country Club brought together sponsors, educators and advocates to fund food, clothing, classroom materials and crisis response year-round.
Teen Living Center Opens
With a ribbon cutting and standing ovation, the TLC officially opened its doors — a community-built home for teens experiencing homelessness or housing insecurity.
Unified Soccer & Chamber Legacy Award
Students of all abilities shared the field, Trunk or Treat packed the lot with community spirit, and the Davis Chamber honored Executive Director Jodi Lunt for turning generosity into impact.
Winter
Elves & the Shoemaker
Led by America First Credit Union, kindergartners were fitted with brand-new shoes. Junior High Child Spree delivered clothing and essentials to older students.
Farmington Teen Center Opens
A safe, steady place for students to land and begin again. Excellence in Education with the Murdock Family Foundation launched to recognize four educators each month across the district.
STEM Expo & No Hunger Zone
Students explored real STEM career pathways. FOX 13 spotlighted the No Hunger Zone and honored volunteers with the Zero Hunger Hero award. Davis High completed the Teen Center network.
Spring
Teacher of the Year & Educator Awards
Kylie Blake earned Outstanding New Educator, Cindy Lemmon received the America First Lifetime Achievement Award, and Kari Harrison was honored with the Mighty Heart Educator of Excellence.
Annual Gala
An evening of gratitude, celebration and storytelling. Record-breaking support raised vital funds for student-centered initiatives and reminded everyone what a community can do together.
Best of Davis & Best of State
The district honored 45 Best of Davis awardees. The Foundation earned four 2025 Best of State medals and was voted Best Charity in Davis County.

With Gratitude to Jodi

Thank You, Jodi Lunt

From 2012 to 2025, Jodi Lunt led Davis Education Foundation through a decade of purpose and growth. She kept the work fixed on one promise: remove barriers so students can stay in school and graduate. Under her leadership, partners rallied, volunteers showed up and support became daily solutions — Teen Resource Centers in every high school, a 16-bed Teen Living Center, pantry packs at scale, practical scholarships and grants that move fast. The result is a stronger, kinder system built to meet students at the moment it matters.

Thank you, Jodi, for the standard you set and the future you helped build.
